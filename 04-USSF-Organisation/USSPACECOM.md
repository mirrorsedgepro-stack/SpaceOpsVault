# USSPACECOM

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Describes USSPACECOM as the combatant command responsible for employing space forces operationally — distinct from USSF, which trains and equips — and its operational hub the Combined Space Operations Center (CSpOC), where ADF personnel embed and where coalition space operations are coordinated.

- **Commander**: Gen Stephen Whiting (also MNF-OOD Commander for [[Operation Olympic Defender]])
- **AOR** begins at 100 km above MSL (the Kármán line)
- Key element: **Combined Space Operations Center (CSpOC)** at Vandenberg SFB

## CSpOC

See [[Coalition Space Operations]] for multinational integration at CSpOC, including [[ADF Space C2|AUSSpOC]], CANSpOC, UK NSPOC, France, Germany, and New Zealand.

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> USSF (the service) trains and equips; USSPACECOM (the combatant command) employs forces — this distinction matters when requesting space support through the joint planning process. Space support requests go to USSPACECOM via the joint fire support or effects coordination process, not to USSF directly. Confusing the two will stall your request.

## Relationship to USSF

- [[USSF Organisation|USSF]] organises, trains, equips
- USSPACECOM **employs forces operationally**
- **S4S** bridges the two as the USSF component to USSPACECOM

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> CSpOC at Vandenberg SFB operates around the clock on watch cycles similar to a ship's bridge. Before arriving for an embed, understand the watch rotation, the reporting chain from the watch floor to the director, and how AUSSpOC fits into the floor structure — arriving informed signals professionalism and shortens your integration time significantly.

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** USSPACECOM is a US combatant command — ADF personnel work within it through liaison and embed arrangements, not as organic members. This limits the authorities ADF officers can exercise independently and affects what classified systems they can access.
>
> **Limitations:** CSpOC's classified common operating picture is not fully accessible to all coalition partners. Some data feeds, threat assessments, and operational overlays are US-eyes-only or Five Eyes only, creating visibility gaps for partners operating alongside US forces.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes USSPACECOM retains primacy for space operations and that coalition command arrangements remain stable. In a major conflict involving multiple theatres, contested command arrangements with allies — particularly around authority to conduct defensive space control — are a realistic friction point.
