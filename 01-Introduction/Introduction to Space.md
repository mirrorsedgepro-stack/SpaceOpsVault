# Introduction to Space

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Establishes why space is a critical military domain and introduces the three core advantages — persistence, global reach, and speed — that make space-based capabilities irreplaceable for modern joint warfare.

## Defining Space

Space begins at **100 km** above mean sea level (the **Kármán line**). Objects in [[Orbital Mechanics|LEO]] travel at 7.8 km/s (28,000 km/h) in continuous freefall — forward velocity balances gravity.

## Three Unique Military Advantages

1. **Persistence** — observe the same region repeatedly via orbital passes
2. **Global Reach** — a constellation covers the entire Earth
3. **Speed** — electromagnetic signals travel at light speed

## US Military Dependence on Space

| Function | Space Enabler |
| --- | --- |
| Command | [[SATCOM Architecture\|SATCOM]] |
| Navigation | [[GPS\|PNT / GPS]] |
| Precision Strike | JDAM, cruise missiles ([[Tomahawk Guidance]]) |
| Threat Detection | [[Missile Warning and Tracking]] |
| Intelligence | [[SIGINT and ELINT Overview\|ISR]] |

> These six core capability areas are **interdependent**. Disrupting any one degrades the others.
> — See [[Military Satellite Capabilities Overview]]

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> The six capability areas are interdependent — an adversary doesn't need to destroy satellites to degrade the joint force, they just need to threaten one link in the chain. When briefing commanders on space threats, frame the risk as a cascading joint effect, not an isolated technical problem.

## Contested Domain

- Space has transitioned from a benign support environment to an **actively contested warfighting domain**
- **Space Situational Awareness (SSA)** was redesignated **[[Space Domain Awareness]]** (SDA) to reflect adversary active attempts to deny, disrupt, degrade, and destroy allied space capabilities
- See [[Counterspace Threats Overview]] for the five threat categories

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> The redesignation from SSA to SDA signals a deliberate doctrinal shift from passive monitoring to active contested warfighting. When working with USSF personnel, use "SDA" — using "SSA" signals unfamiliarity with current doctrine and culture.

## United States Space Force

- [[USSF Organisation|USSF]] established **20 December 2019** as the sixth branch of the US armed services
- Members called **Guardians**

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Most space capabilities described here are US-controlled — ADF access is governed by bilateral agreements and treaty arrangements, and can be limited or conditioned in a crisis.
>
> **Limitations:** The "global reach" advantage assumes satellites are not being actively jammed, spoofed, or manoeuvred against. In a contested environment, all three advantages degrade simultaneously.
>
> **Assumptions:** This note assumes adversaries have not yet achieved effective space denial at the outset of a conflict scenario. The picture changes significantly if an adversary achieves even partial space denial capability.
