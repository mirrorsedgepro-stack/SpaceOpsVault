# Space Doctrine

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Maps the doctrinal framework governing US space operations — from the foundational Spacepower 2020 concept through to the operational publications that guide SDA, targeting, and space warfighting — and identifies the key documents ADF personnel need to understand before working with USSF.

## Key Publications

| Publication | Year | Significance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **JP 3-14 Space Operations** | Aug 2023 | Foundational joint publication; codified [[USSPACECOM]] scope for offensive and defensive operations; introduced astrographic AOR concept; normalised space as warfighting domain; limited distribution (DoD only) |
| **Spacepower 2020** | 2020 | Space Capstone Publication — top of [[USSF Organisation\|USSF]] doctrine hierarchy |
| **SDP 3-0 Operations** | 2023 | Keystone USSF operations doctrine |
| **SDP 3-100 SDA** | 2023 | [[Space Domain Awareness]] doctrine |
| **SDP 3-101 Targeting** | 2024 | [[Space-Based Targeting]] doctrine |
| **Space Warfighting: A Framework for Planners** | April 2025 | Key document marking USSF's shift to a warfighting service; defines space superiority and offensive/defensive space operations. |
| **SDP 3-99 JADO** | 2021 | Joint all-domain operations |

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> JP 3-14 is limited distribution (DoD only) — ADF personnel cannot simply download it. Request access through your chain of command before deploying with USSF, and allow lead time for releasability approval. Arriving without having read JP 3-14 limits your effectiveness in joint planning immediately.

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> As of 2026, "Space Warfighting: A Framework for Planners" is the baseline for "Dynamic Space Operations." It marks the cultural shift where Guardians identify as warfighters first. Reading this is essential for aligning with USSF's focus on "competitive endurance" and "space superiority."

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** JP 3-14 is DoD-only and subject to ITAR/releasability controls. ADF access to the full operational doctrine suite requires formal approval — some documents may be withheld or provided in sanitised form even to Five Eyes partners.
>
> **Limitations:** Doctrine lags technology and organisational change. The USSF is evolving faster than its published doctrine — field practices at CSpOC and within Deltas may diverge from what is written. Treat doctrine as baseline, not current state.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes US and ADF doctrine are broadly aligned. In practice there are gaps — particularly around authorities, rules of engagement, and definitions of hostile acts in space — that must be identified and managed at the operational level before a crisis.
