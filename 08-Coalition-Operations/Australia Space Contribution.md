# Australia's Unique Space Contribution

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Catalogues Australia's concrete contributions to coalition space operations — SST Exmouth, Kojarena MUOS facility, WGS-6 funding, and Pine Gap — explaining why Australia is considered a Tier 1 space partner. This context underpins ADF's standing in every coalition space negotiation.

Australia is one of the most **deeply embedded** US space partners.

## Key Contributions

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Australia's space contribution is strategically disproportionate to its size — Pine Gap alone is one of the most important intelligence facilities in the world. When engaging with USSF counterparts, be aware they view ADF as a genuinely valued partner, not a dependent. This gives ADF more negotiating leverage than many assume.

## Kojarena (SGS-West), Western Australia

- Can anchor **three WGS satellites** simultaneously
- Hosts MUOS Radio Access Facility
- ADSCS (ECHELON) satellite intercept station
- See [[Five Eyes SIGINT Network]]

## Pine Gap, Alice Springs

- Controls Advanced Orion GEO SIGINT satellites
- SATCOM/sat phone/microwave intercept
- Geolocation for military targeting
- Joint ASD/NSA/NRO/CIA staffing with **Australian Deputy Chief**

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Pine Gap, Kojarena, and SST Exmouth are covered by bilateral agreements that constrain how Australia can discuss them publicly.
>
> **Limitations:** Australia's sovereign satellite capability is limited — ADF depends on US and commercial satellites for most space-based effects.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes the bilateral agreements underpinning these facilities remain stable; assumes US willingness to fund continued joint operations at shared sites.
