# ADF EW Platforms

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Catalogues the ADF's key EW-capable platforms — EA-18G Growler, E-7A Wedgetail, F-35A, and Hobart-class DDG — and their roles in the space-integrated EMSO mission. Knowing platform capabilities and limitations is essential for realistic joint planning.

| Platform | Key Sensor/EW System | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **E-7A Wedgetail** | Northrop Grumman MESA L-band AESA (600+ km range) | AEW&C, C2, AMTI |
| **EA-18G Growler** | AN/APG-79 AESA + ALQ-99 | Airborne electronic attack; RAAF only non-US operator |
| **F-35A** | AN/APG-81 AESA + AN/ASQ-239 (360° passive ELINT) | Stealth sensor, EW suite |
| **Hobart-class DDG** | CEA CEAFAR/CEAMOUNT (Australian-developed S-band AESA) + Aegis | AAW, missile defence |
| **Hunter-class FFG** | CEA CEAFAR 2 (next-gen) | Future AAW |
| **JORN** | HF over-the-horizon radar (1,000–3,000 km) | Long-range surveillance |

## Key Notes

- **EA-18G Growler**: RAAF is the **only non-US operator** worldwide (No. 6 Squadron, 11 aircraft)
- **F-35A AN/ASQ-239**: provides **360° passive ELINT** — every F-35A sortie generates ELINT data
- **CEAFAR**: Australian-developed phased-array radar — sovereign ADF technology
- **JORN**: HF OTH radar providing strategic-depth surveillance across northern approaches

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> The EA-18G Growler is Australia's only dedicated electronic attack aircraft — it is a scarce, high-demand asset. In coalition planning, expect intense competition for Growler sorties. If your plan depends on EW support, get it in writing early in the planning cycle.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** ADF Growlers are a small fleet — their availability for any given operation depends on maintenance cycles and competing taskings. Export control restrictions limit some Growler capabilities to US-only employment.
>
> **Limitations:** Airborne EW platforms must operate in the threat environment — Growler survivability depends on fighter escort and threat suppression.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes ADF EW platforms maintain their current capability edge — adversary advances in counter-EW are ongoing.
