# SIGINT Satellites by Nation

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Surveys the SIGINT satellite constellations operated by the US (Orion, NOSS), Russia (Lotos, Olymp), and China (Yaogan) — covering their orbital characteristics and collection capabilities. Understanding who has eyes and ears in orbit is essential for communications security and operational planning.

| System | Nation | Type | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Advanced Orion** | USA (NRO) | GEO SIGINT | ~78m unfurlable antenna; L–Ku band; controlled from [[Australia Space Contribution\|Pine Gap]] and Menwith Hill |
| **NOSS** | USA (NRO) | LEO triplets | TDOA geolocation of ship radars |
| **Yaogan ELINT series** | China | LEO triplets | ~40 naval ELINT satellites; detect/geolocate ship radars via TDOA; feeds DF-21D/DF-26 [[Kill Chain Comparison\|kill chain]] |
| **Shijian-20** | China | GEO SIGINT | Most advanced Chinese SIGINT satellite; covers Asia-Pacific |
| **Lotos-S1** (14F145) | Russia | LEO passive ELINT | Replaces Soviet US-P/US-PM |
| **Pion-NKS** (14F139) | Russia | LEO active radar | Ocean reconnaissance; complements Lotos |
| **Olymp-K / Luch** | Russia | GEO SIGINT | Conducts [[Orbital Mechanics\|RPO]] near Western GEO SATCOM; intercepts downlink traffic |

## Key Implications

- **Yaogan triplets** (~40 satellites): provide China with persistent maritime ELINT coverage — can detect and geolocate carrier strike group radar emissions via TDOA, feeding the DF-21D/DF-26 anti-ship ballistic missile kill chain
- **Advanced Orion** (~78m antenna): among the largest satellites ever launched; provides strategic SIGINT from GEO
- **Olymp-K**: combines SIGINT with provocative [[Orbital Mechanics|RPO]] — dual intelligence/counterspace function

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> NOSS (Naval Ocean Surveillance System) triplets use a distinctive formation that is detectable in Space-Track data — if you see three satellites in a tight trailing formation in LEO, that's NOSS. Knowing what SIGINT satellites are overhead matters for communications security planning.
