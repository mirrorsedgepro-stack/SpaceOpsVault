# SIGINT and ELINT Overview

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Introduces the SIGINT disciplines — COMINT, ELINT, and FISINT — and how they contribute to building the Electronic Order of Battle (EOB) that underpins targeting and threat warning. The EOB is the essential intelligence product that enables every downstream EW and targeting effect.

## Disciplines

| Discipline | Full Name | Collects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **COMINT** | Communications Intelligence | Voice, data, fax |
| **ELINT** | Electronic Intelligence | Radar, missile guidance, navigation emitters |
| **FISINT** | Foreign Instrumentation Signals Intelligence | Weapons tests, missile telemetry |

## Electronic Order of Battle (EOB)

- Catalogue of adversary emitters
- **Cannot jam/deceive systems not characterised** — EOB is prerequisite for effective EA
- Built through persistent [[Electronic Warfare Fundamentals|ES/ESM]] collection

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> ELINT is the foundation for jamming — you can only jam what you know about. Building an EOB before an operation is not optional; it's what enables every EA effect that follows. If your ELINT picture is incomplete, your EW plan has gaps.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** SIGINT is a restricted intelligence discipline — collection authorities and dissemination rules are tightly controlled and vary by classification. ADF SIGINT products flow through specific channels and are not available to all staff.
>
> **Limitations:** SIGINT is a snapshot — an adversary that changes frequencies, encryption, or emission patterns can quickly degrade an EOB built on prior collection.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes adversaries are not aware of collection activities and have not deliberately fed false signals to mislead EOB analysis (electronic deception).
