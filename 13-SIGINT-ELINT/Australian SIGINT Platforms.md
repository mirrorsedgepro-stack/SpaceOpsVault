# Australian SIGINT Platforms

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Covers Australia's primary airborne SIGINT platforms — MC-55A Peregrine, EA-18G, P-8A, and Collins-class — and their contributions to the Five Eyes collection architecture. ADF SIGINT platforms are a direct Australian contribution to the alliance intelligence picture, not just consumers of it.

## MC-55A Peregrine (No. 10 Sqn)

- **4 on order**; $1.6B (AIR 555)
- **First delivered January 2026**
- COMINT: 30–1200 MHz
- ELINT: 0.5–40 GHz
- Ceiling: 51,000 ft; endurance: 15 hours
- **Networking relay**: links F-35A/E-7A/EA-18G/surface combatants
- Uses [[Tactical Data Links Overview|CDL]] for ISR data transfer

## EA-18G Growler (No. 6 Sqn)

- **11 aircraft**; primary airborne electronic attack
- Generates ELINT targeting data
- RAAF is **only non-US operator**

## P-8A Poseidon (No. 11 Sqn)

- **15 aircraft**
- Maritime ESM/ELINT as secondary role

## Collins-class Submarine

- Periscope-mounted ESM
- Future **SSN-AUKUS** will carry ESM

## Adversary Collection Against ADF

> **Chinese Type 815A** intelligence ship observed near Talisman Sabre — collects ADF radar emissions and tactical procedures.

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> The MC-55A Peregrine is Australia's newest and most capable airborne SIGINT platform — it significantly increases ADF's organic collection capability in the Indo-Pacific. Understanding what it can collect matters for planning; understanding its vulnerabilities (it is not a stealth aircraft) matters for force protection.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** ADF SIGINT platform capabilities are sensitive — specific collection parameters and tasking authorities are classified. Platforms operate under strict legal frameworks governing SIGINT collection against specific target categories.
>
> **Limitations:** Airborne SIGINT platforms are limited by flight hours, crew endurance, and basing access. The Indo-Pacific's vast distances create significant coverage gaps even with persistent tasking.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes basing access and overflight permissions for collection orbits remain available — in a crisis, these may be politically constrained.
