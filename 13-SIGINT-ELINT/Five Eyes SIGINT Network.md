# Five Eyes SIGINT Network

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Describes the Five Eyes SIGINT-sharing architecture — UKUSA Agreement, STONEGHOST network, and the key Australian collection sites (Pine Gap, Kojarena) — and their role in providing global SIGINT coverage. ADF personnel operating in joint environments will routinely consume intelligence derived from this architecture.

## Key Stations

| Station | Location | Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pine Gap** | Alice Springs, AUS 🇦🇺 | Controls [[SIGINT Satellites by Nation\|Advanced Orion]]; SATCOM/sat phone/microwave intercept; geolocation for military targeting; joint ASD/NSA/NRO/CIA with Australian Deputy Chief |
| **ADSCS Kojarena** | Geraldton, WA 🇦🇺 | ECHELON satellite intercept + [[SATCOM Architecture\|WGS]]/[[SATCOM Architecture\|MUOS]] ground station |
| **Shoal Bay** | Darwin, AUS 🇦🇺 | HF/UHF SIGINT Southeast Asia |
| **Menwith Hill** | Yorkshire, UK 🇬🇧 | Largest NSA site outside US; GEO control for Europe/Middle East |

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Pine Gap is arguably Australia's single most strategically important intelligence asset — it provides missile warning, satellite tracking, and SIGINT collection that is central to US global operations. ADF personnel operating in joint environments should understand that Pine Gap outputs often inform the intelligence products they receive, even when the source is not stated.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Five Eyes SIGINT sharing is governed by strict need-to-know and compartment controls — even within FVEY, not all intelligence is shared with all partners at all classification levels.
>
> **Limitations:** Five Eyes architecture was designed around Cold War Soviet threat — adaptation to Chinese and other threats has been ongoing.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes Five Eyes intelligence-sharing relationships remain stable — political friction between member nations can affect the flow of sensitive intelligence.
