# 18th Space Defense Squadron

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Describes the 18th Space Defense Squadron — the USSF unit responsible for maintaining the space object catalogue and issuing conjunction warnings globally, including to commercial operators and allied nations. Understanding their role and outputs is essential for any space operations planning involving conjunction risk.

- Maintains the **space catalogue** (44,000+ objects)
- Issues **conjunction warnings worldwide** for dangerous close approaches
- Operates [[ATLAS]]
- Assigned to **Space Delta 2** under [[USSF Organisation|CFC]]
- Location: **Vandenberg SFB**

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> The 18th SDS issues public conjunction warnings and re-entry predictions — these are available via Space-Track to any registered user. Before an exercise or operation, check Space-Track for any high-interest conjunction events that might affect planning windows.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** The 18th SDS maintains two catalogues — the unclassified one on Space-Track and a classified one that includes objects not publicly acknowledged. ADF access to the classified picture is via coalition arrangements.
>
> **Limitations:** Conjunction warnings are probabilistic — a low probability of collision (e.g., 1 in 1,000) still requires a manoeuvre decision under uncertainty. Turnaround time from detection to warning varies.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes the 18th SDS has catalogued all relevant objects — uncatalogued debris (especially from DA-ASAT events) creates residual risk.
