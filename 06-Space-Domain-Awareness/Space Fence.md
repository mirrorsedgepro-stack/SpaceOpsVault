# Space Fence

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Details the Space Fence S-band radar at Kwajalein Atoll — the most sensitive ground-based LEO tracking sensor in the US network, capable of detecting marble-sized objects. It significantly expanded the trackable catalogue and is the primary sensor for debris conjunction warnings in LEO.

- **Type**: S-band phased-array radar
- **Location**: Kwajalein Atoll, Marshall Islands
- **Contractor**: Lockheed Martin
- **Operational**: 2020
- **Capability**: detects **marble-sized objects** in [[Orbital Mechanics|LEO]]
- Contributes to the Space Surveillance Network (SSN)
- Critical for conjunction assessment and debris tracking

> Space Fence dramatically increased the number of trackable objects in LEO, contributing to the 44,000+ catalogued objects maintained by the [[18th Space Defense Squadron]].

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Space Fence detects but does not continuously track — it builds a picture through multiple passes. A conjunction warning from Space Fence for a new small fragment may take several orbits to refine. Don't expect immediate precision on newly detected objects.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Space Fence data feeds into the classified catalogue first — unclassified Space-Track entries for Space Fence detections may lag hours to days.
>
> **Limitations:** Space Fence covers LEO effectively but provides limited MEO/GEO coverage — deep-space tracking relies on other systems (GEODSS, GSSAP). It cannot detect objects that do not enter its radar beam.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes Kwajalein ground infrastructure remains operational — it is a single point of failure for this sensor. Assumes adversaries are not using radar-absorbent materials on key satellites.
