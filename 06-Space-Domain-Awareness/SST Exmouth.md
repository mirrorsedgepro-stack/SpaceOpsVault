# SST (Space Surveillance Telescope) — Exmouth, WA

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Covers the C-band Space Surveillance Telescope at Exmouth, Western Australia — Australia's primary contribution to the global SDA sensor network, providing unique coverage of the southern sky deep-space belt. This is the key asset that gives ADF personnel direct relevance to the global space catalogue mission.

- **Type**: Wide-field telescope for deep-space surveillance
- **Location**: Exmouth, Western Australia
- **Operator**: RAAF ([[ADF Space C2|1SSU]])
- **Operational**: since 2022
- Relocated from the United States
- **Second** Southern Hemisphere sensor in the Space Surveillance Network (SSN)
- Provides **critical deep-space coverage of the southern sky** — a gap no other allied sensor fills

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> SST Exmouth fills a genuine coverage gap — no other sensor in the network covers the southern hemisphere GEO belt as well. This makes Australia's contribution strategically significant; losing SST Exmouth would create a blind spot that adversaries could exploit.

Part of [[Australia Space Contribution]] to the SSN and [[Coalition Space Operations]].

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** SST Exmouth is operated by the RAAF under a US-Australia agreement — tasking authority and data sharing protocols are governed by that agreement.
>
> **Limitations:** Optical telescopes like SST are degraded by cloud cover, daylight, and atmospheric turbulence — it cannot provide 24/7 coverage in all weather.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes the Exmouth site remains accessible and secure; the remote location creates logistics and security considerations in a heightened threat environment.
