# Link 22

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Covers Link 22 — the HF/UHF beyond-line-of-sight tactical data link designed to complement Link 16 for maritime and dispersed operations, with inherent self-healing network architecture. Its SATCOM-independent BLOS capability makes it operationally relevant wherever satellite access cannot be guaranteed.

- **Standard**: STANAG 5522
- **Frequencies**: HF (2–30 MHz) and UHF (225–400 MHz)
- **Range**: **BLOS 1,000+ nm via HF sky-wave** — critical for Indo-Pacific distances
- **Access method**: Dynamic TDMA; self-healing network
- **Replaces**: Link 11 (NATO)

## Operational Testing

- French Navy tested operationally with Charles de Gaulle CSG during Indo-Pacific deployment (February 2025)

## ADF Relevance

- Link 22 integration planned for RAN surface combatants
- Complements [[Link 16]] for the vast Indo-Pacific distances where LOS is insufficient
- Vulnerable to [[Russian EW Systems|Murmansk-BN]] HF jammer (5,000 km range)

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Link 22's HF capability gives it BLOS range without satellite relay — in a scenario where SATCOM is degraded, Link 22 becomes more valuable, not less. It's the anti-fragile data link. Ensure maritime operators understand its role as a SATCOM-independent BLOS option.

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Link 22 is less widely fielded than Link 16 — check which coalition platforms support it before planning Link 22 as the primary BLOS link.
>
> **Limitations:** HF propagation is affected by ionospheric conditions — solar events and time-of-day affect link reliability. HF data rates are lower than Link 16's VHF/UHF channels.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes ionospheric conditions support HF propagation — in polar or post-nuclear environments, HF propagation can be severely disrupted.
