# Golden Dome Missile Defence (March 2026 Update)

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Tracks the Golden Dome missile defence initiative — an executive-order-driven programme to build a multi-layer space-based interceptor and sensor architecture for homeland defence, with a $185B projected cost as of March 2026.

## Overview

- Proposed multi-layer missile defence system to detect and destroy ballistic, hypersonic, and cruise missiles
- Signed by executive order **January 2025**
- Programme manager: Gen Michael Guetlein ([[USSF Organisation|USSF]])

## Cost

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Golden Dome's cost estimate has already increased by $60B from initial estimates — treat all projected timelines and costs with appropriate scepticism. The programme is real, but the delivery schedule is highly dependent on political and industrial factors that are still evolving.

- Initial estimate: $125B (Trump, May 2025)
- **Revised estimate: $185B** (March 2026) — up $10B to accelerate space capabilities
- Congress approved $25B through FY2025 reconciliation bill
- Projected completion: **2035** (six years later than original timeline)

## Key Components

- Space-based sensors and tracking (leverages [[SDA Transport Layer (PWSA)|PWSA]])
- Boost-phase space-based interceptor prototypes (contracted Nov 2025)
- [[GMTI and AMTI|AMTI]] acceleration
- Space Data Network
- Hypersonic and Ballistic Tracking Space Sensor (HBTSS)

## C2 Consortium

Nine companies with deals for Golden Dome C2 layer including Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, and Raytheon.

## DOT&E Concerns

March 2026 report notes the system would require demonstrations under ideal conditions by end of 2028 before any operational capability.

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Golden Dome details are subject to rapid change — programme architecture, contractor selection, and cost are all in flux as of March 2026.
>
> **Limitations:** This note reflects March 2026 reporting — monitor Breaking Defense and SpaceNews for updates. The DOT&E assessment that operational capability before 2028 demonstrations is not credible should be factored into planning assumptions.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes Congressional funding remains available — Golden Dome is politically contingent on sustained executive and legislative support.
