# SDA Tranche 1 Launch Campaign (March 2026 Update)

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Tracks the SDA Proliferated Warfighter Space Architecture Tranche 1 launch campaign — the initial operational satellites providing BLOS Link 16 relay and missile tracking, with 10 more launches planned in 2026.

## Timeline

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Check sda.mil/news before any planning discussion involving PWSA — the Tranche 1 launch schedule has slipped multiple times and the on-orbit count directly affects coverage assumptions. Don't plan as though full Tranche 1 is operational when only a portion of the constellation is up.

| Date | Event |
| --- | --- |
| Jun 2025 | T1DES prototype satellite launched (York Space Systems pathfinder) |
| **10 Sep 2025** | First T1 Transport Layer batch: 21 York Space Systems satellites |
| **15 Oct 2025** | Second batch: 21 Lockheed Martin satellites |
| Late 2025 | Launches paused (vendor readiness + facility issues) |
| Early 2026 | Launches resumed; Northrop Grumman T1TL set for early 2026 |
| 2026 | **10 launches planned**: 6 Transport + 4 Tracking |

## Full Constellation

- **154 operational satellites** when complete: 126 Transport + 28 Tracking
- Orbit altitude: ~1,000 km
- Average cost per Transport satellite: ~$14M

## Initial Users

- **Indo-Pacific Command** forces designated as first operational users
- Capability: BLOS [[Link 16]] relay, advanced missile tracking, missile warning

## Tranche 2 and 3

- **Tranche 2**: 550+ satellites; first launches expected late 2026/early 2027
- **Tranche 3 Tracking**: 72 satellites; $3.5B contracted to L3Harris, Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, Rocket Lab (Dec 2025)

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** PWSA operational capability is currently limited to the satellites on orbit — coverage is not yet global.
>
> **Limitations:** This note reflects launch status as of early 2026 — satellite health, on-orbit checkouts, and actual coverage may differ from the planned schedule.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes vendors (York, Lockheed, Northrop) meet their 2026 launch windows — historically optimistic; check current status before planning.
