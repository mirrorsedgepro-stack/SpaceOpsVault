# National Defense Strategy 2026

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Covers the 2026 National Defence Strategy's hemispheric security concept and how it positions space — particularly Golden Dome — as central to US homeland defence posture.

## Key Shifts

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> The NDS 2026 frames space as a homeland defence domain, not just an expeditionary enabler — this is a significant doctrinal shift. When engaging with USSF planners, expect to hear homeland defence as a first-tier mission, which affects how resources are allocated between homeland and forward operations.

- Introduces **Hemispheric Security** concept
- [[Golden Dome Missile Defence|Golden Dome]] central to homeland defence posture
- De-prioritises European continent security
- Elevates space-enabled missile defence as cornerstone of strategy

## Implications for ADF

- US focus on homeland and hemisphere may increase demands on allied self-sufficiency
- Reinforces importance of [[ADF SATCOM Systems|sovereign SATCOM]] and [[ADF Space C2|Space Command]] investment
- [[Coalition Space Operations|Operation Olympic Defender]] and Year of Integration take on additional significance as alliance integration mechanism

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** NDS details beyond the public summary are classified — the full strategy has implications not reflected in open-source reporting.
>
> **Limitations:** Strategy documents lag operational reality — USSF behaviour in 2026 may be ahead of what NDS 2026 formally articulates.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes NDS 2026 reflects the enduring priorities of the current administration — policy changes can redirect emphasis rapidly.
