# ATLAS and Kronos C2 (March 2026 Update)

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Covers the ATLAS SDA software (declared operational September 2025) and the upcoming Kronos space C2 system — the two most important C2 transitions reshaping how the USSF manages its space operations picture.

## ATLAS Status

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> ATLAS replaced SPADOC but DOT&E said SPADOC cannot yet be fully decommissioned — during exercises and early operational periods, be alert to which system produced a given SDA product. Data quality between the two systems may differ, especially for legacy sensor feeds.

- **Operational acceptance**: September 2025
- **DOT&E finding (March 2026)**: lacks minimum viable capability to decommission SPADOC
- Deficiencies consistent with **system immaturity**
- SPADOC no longer used "operationally" but decommission plan still in development

## Kronos — New C2 Component

- Space Force restructured Space C2 programme (May 2025) into two parallel tracks:
  - **[[ATLAS]]**: foundational [[Space Domain Awareness|SDA]] and data processing
  - **Kronos**: battlespace awareness, theatre support, active space defence
- First Kronos contracts expected **April 2026** via Other Transactions
- Three lines of effort with staggered awards

## Implications

The split acknowledges that basic SDA data processing ([[ATLAS]]) and active warfighting C2 (Kronos) require different development approaches and timelines.

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** ATLAS is classified — ADF access is through coalition channels, not direct system access. Kronos is in development and not yet fielded.
>
> **Limitations:** ATLAS is new with limited operational history — its behaviour under high-tempo space event conditions (multiple conjunctions, adversary manoeuvres) is not fully characterised.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes L3Harris has fully integrated all legacy sensor feeds into ATLAS — gaps in integration would manifest as missing tracks relative to the SPADOC picture.
