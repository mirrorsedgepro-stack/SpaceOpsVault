# Interactive Tools and Resources

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> A curated directory of publicly accessible SDA tools, news sources, think tanks, and education platforms — the go-to reference for hands-on exploration and staying current with military space developments.

## Space Tracking and Visualization

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Register for Space-Track.org before you need it — the registration approval process can take days. Don't find yourself in an exercise or operation needing orbital data and without an account. Registration is free and open to non-US nationals.

### Space-Track.org
- **URL:** https://www.space-track.org
- Operated by the USSF [[18th Space Defense Squadron]]. The authoritative source for unclassified orbital data — TLEs, OMMs, the satellite catalogue (27,000+ objects), conjunction data messages (CDMs), and re-entry predictions.
- Free account required; open to non-US users with registration.

### CelesTrak
- **URL:** https://celestrak.org
- Non-profit run by Dr T.S. Kelso. Republishes and supplements Space-Track data. Hosts **SOCRATES** (Satellite Orbital Conjunction Reports Assessing Threatening Encounters in Space) for collision risk analysis.

### KeepTrack
- **URL:** https://keeptrack.space / https://celestrak.keeptrack.space
- Free, open-source browser-based 3D orbital viewer fed from CelesTrak data. Shows 30,000+ catalogued objects in real time. Features include simulated launches, breakup visualisation, and conjunction analysis. Best free training tool available.

### LeoLabs
- **URL:** https://leolabs.space / visualization: https://platform.leolabs.space/visualization
- Commercial ground-based radar network providing LEO tracking, conjunction analysis, satellite characterisation, and re-entry prediction. Public visualization is free; full data platform requires subscription. Serves government, commercial, and defence customers.

### ASTRIAGraph
- **URL:** http://astria.tacc.utexas.edu/AstriaGraph
- University of Texas at Austin (Prof. Moriba Jah). Near real-time 3D map of 26,000+ objects aggregating six independent data sources (including Space-Track and commercial providers). Useful for comparing how different tracking networks diverge on the same object.

### Stuff in Space
- **URL:** https://stuffin.space
- Clean WebGL 3D viewer of all catalogued objects, updated daily from Space-Track data. Searchable by name or NORAD ID.

### Heavens-Above
- **URL:** https://www.heavens-above.com
- Location-based pass prediction for ISS, Starlink, and other bright satellites. Enter coordinates to get precise overpass times for naked-eye observation. Useful for practical sky-watching exercises.

### N2YO
- **URL:** https://www.n2yo.com
- Real-time satellite tracking with live ground tracks and pass predictions for 16,000+ satellites. Free.

## Simulation Software

### Ansys STK (Systems Tool Kit)
- **URL:** https://www.ansys.com/products/missions/ansys-stk
- Industry-standard multi-domain simulation used by NASA, DoD, Lockheed Martin, and Northrop Grumman. Models satellites, ground stations, sensors, access windows, and coverage analysis. Free 14-day trial available; academic licences via the Ansys Academic Partner Programme.
- **Training:** L1–L3 certification at ansys.com/training-center. Level 1 is accessible to newcomers.

## Think Tanks and Research Organisations

### CSIS Aerospace Security Project
- **URL:** https://aerospace.csis.org
- Publishes the annual **Space Threat Assessment** — the most-cited unclassified analysis of global counterspace capabilities (China, Russia, Iran, North Korea).
- Latest: [Space Threat Assessment 2025](https://www.csis.org/analysis/space-threat-assessment-2025)

### Secure World Foundation (SWF)
- **URL:** https://www.swfound.org
- Publishes the annual **Global Counterspace Capabilities: An Open Source Assessment** — the most comprehensive unclassified catalogue of counterspace weapons (directed energy, ASAT, cyber, EW, RPO). 316 pages in 2025 edition.
- [2025 Report](https://www.swfound.org/publications-and-reports/2025-global-counterspace-capabilities-report)

### RAND Corporation
- **URL:** https://www.rand.org/topics/space-warfare.html
- Policy-relevant research on space security, deterrence, and warfighting.
- Notable: [Lessons from the War in Ukraine for Space (2025)](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA2950-1.html)

### Mitchell Institute for Aerospace Studies
- **URL:** https://www.mitchellaerospacepower.org
- AFA think tank. Practitioner-oriented papers on space power and USSF roles.
- Notable: [Ensuring a Spacepower Advantage in Prolonged Competition (Feb 2025)](https://www.mitchellaerospacepower.org/app/uploads/2025/02/Ensuring-a-Spacepower-Advantage-in-Prolonged-Competition-FINAL-WEB.pdf)

### Aerospace Corporation — Center for Space Policy and Strategy (CSPS)
- **URL:** https://csps.aerospace.org
- FFRDC advising the U.S. government. Technical-level space policy analysis. Hosts the **Space Policy Show** podcast.
- [Space Agenda 2025](https://csps.aerospace.org/sa2025)

## Education and Training

### NSSI — National Security Space Institute (USSF)
- **URL:** https://nssi.spaceforce.mil
- Primary USSF professional military education institution. Online and resident courses for military and allied personnel covering orbital mechanics, SDA, space warfighting, and space systems. Allied access available to ADF personnel.

### STARCOM — Space Training and Readiness Command
- **URL:** https://www.starcom.spaceforce.mil
- USSF command responsible for all space training, doctrine, and education. Oversees NSSI.

### ADF JCO-200 Course
- Operated by [[ADF Space C2|1 Space Surveillance Unit]], Adelaide.
- First international delivery: September 2025. Covers orbital mechanics, space warfare, space weather, RPO, predictive surveillance, launches, and re-entry events.
- Contact through ADF Space Command channels.

### EPFL — Space Mission Design and Operations
- **URL:** https://www.edx.org (search "Space Mission Design EPFL")
- Free-to-audit MOOC from the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology. One of the most rigorous publicly available space operations courses. Covers spacecraft design, orbital mechanics, mission operations, and satellite systems.
