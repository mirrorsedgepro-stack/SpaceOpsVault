# China (PRC) — Most Comprehensive Counterspace Threat

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Details China's counterspace capabilities across all five threat categories — from DA-ASAT demonstrated in 2007 to active RPO programmes and directed energy systems — situating China as the most capable peer adversary in space. Essential reading for any planner operating in the Indo-Pacific strategic environment.

## Force Organisation

- Created **Information Support Force** in 2024 (disbanded Strategic Support Force)
- Deeper integration of space/cyber/EW under unified command

## Satellite Fleet

- Operational satellite fleet exceeded **1,060** by mid-2025
- Accelerating launch rates
- When paired with AI processing: near-real-time global target detection including naval vessels and force movements
- See [[SIGINT Satellites by Nation]] for Yaogan ELINT constellation (~40 naval ELINT satellites)

## Counterspace Capabilities

| Capability | Status |
| --- | --- |
| **DA-ASAT** | Demonstrated 2007 (Fengyun-1C); operational |
| **Ground-based laser ASAT** | Possesses capability; dazzle/blind sensors |
| **GEO jamming satellites** | Reports of experimental capability |
| **Co-orbital / RPO** | SJ-series demonstrating advanced manoeuvring |
| **GPS/SATCOM jamming** | PLA exercises regularly incorporate jammers |
| **Cyber** | Persistent targeting of space ground infrastructure |

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> China's 2007 Fengyun-1C ASAT test created 3,000+ fragments that are still in orbit — this debris field remains a hazard for every LEO operator today. China demonstrated capability 18 years ago; assume they have improved significantly since.

## Operational Implications

- China's kill chain uses **non-Western infrastructure** ([[BeiDou B3A and SMC|BeiDou]]/Yaogan/Gaofen) — Western GPS denial has **zero effect**
- See [[Kill Chain Comparison]] for China's DF-21D/DF-26 anti-ship kill chain
- [[BeiDou B3A and SMC|BeiDou B3A]] frequency-hopping makes Chinese military GNSS essentially unjammable by current Western EW

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Much of China's counterspace capability is assessed from open-source and signals intelligence — specific capabilities and readiness levels are classified.
>
> **Limitations:** Chinese counterspace doctrine is not fully understood; intentions cannot be read with certainty from capabilities.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes China's space capabilities continue to grow at current rates — key unknown is how quickly directed energy systems are maturing.
