# Counterspace Threats Overview

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Maps the five counterspace threat categories — kinetic DA-ASAT, directed energy, electronic warfare, cyber, and co-orbital RPO — and the key events that have shaped the current threat landscape. This note is the essential starting point for understanding what adversaries can do to space systems and the assets that depend on them.

## Five Threat Categories

| Category | Description | Key Actors |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Kinetic (DA-ASAT)** | Direct-ascent missiles physically destroy satellites; creates persistent debris | US, China, Russia, India |
| **Non-Kinetic (Directed Energy)** | Lasers dazzle/blind sensors; HPM damages electronics; no debris | China, Russia |
| **Electronic Warfare** | Jamming overwhelms signals; spoofing injects false data | Russia, China, Iran |
| **Cyber** | Attacks ground stations, data links, satellite software | All state actors |
| **Co-orbital / RPO** | Manoeuvre satellite near target for intelligence, positioning, or attack | China, Russia |

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Electronic warfare (jamming/spoofing) is the most frequently employed counterspace capability — it's cheap, deniable, and reversible. When assessing threat activity, EW effects are far more likely day-to-day than kinetic events. Don't fixate on DA-ASAT; the daily threat is in the electromagnetic spectrum.

## Key DA-ASAT Events

| Year | Actor | Event | Debris |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2007 | China | Destroyed Fengyun-1C | 3,000+ fragments (still present) |
| 2008 | USA | Operation Burnt Frost | Minimal (low orbit) |
| 2019 | India | Mission Shakti | ~400 fragments, mostly decayed |
| 2021 | Russia | Destroyed Cosmos 1408 | 1,500+ fragments |

- **US moratorium April 2022**: committed not to conduct destructive DA-ASAT testing
- No destructive kinetic ASAT tests since Russia 2021

## Normalisation of Sub-Threshold Activity

> **Daily occurrence**: cyber attacks, [[GPS]] jamming, unfriendly [[Orbital Mechanics|RPO]], EW — rarely trigger escalatory responses.

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> "Sub-threshold" activity — GPS jamming, unfriendly RPO, cyber probes — occurs daily without triggering escalatory responses. Adversaries have learned that this grey zone is effective. When briefing commanders, frame EW activity as tactical not existential, but cumulative.

> [!tip]- Answers *(click to reveal)*
> 1. **Kinetic DA-ASAT**, **Non-Kinetic Directed Energy**, **Electronic Warfare**, **Cyber**, **Co-orbital RPO**
> 2. **Electronic Warfare** (jamming and spoofing) — cheap, deniable, and reversible. GPS jamming occurs daily in multiple conflict zones
> 3. Over **3,000 fragments** from Fengyun-1C — many are still in orbit today as an ongoing hazard
> 4. The US committed **not to conduct destructive DA-ASAT testing** against satellites — signalling restraint while retaining existing capability
> 5. It achieves strategic effects (degrading adversary capabilities, probing defences, normalising aggression) without crossing the threshold that would trigger a formal escalatory response — adversaries have learned the grey zone works

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Attribution of counterspace attacks is politically sensitive — many events are acknowledged years after the fact. Publicly stating an adversary conducted an attack requires intelligence community and government coordination.
>
> **Limitations:** The five categories overlap — a cyber attack on a ground station is also a counterspace attack; spoofing is both cyber and EW. Categories are analytical tools, not mutually exclusive.
>
> **Assumptions:** The threat assessments in this note reflect open-source reporting — the classified picture is more detailed and more concerning.
