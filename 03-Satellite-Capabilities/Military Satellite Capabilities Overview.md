# Military Satellite Capabilities Overview

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Introduces the six core military satellite capability areas — the foundational taxonomy used throughout this vault. Understanding these categories is essential before engaging with any detailed capability or vulnerability material.

## Six Core Capability Areas

1. **[[GPS|PNT (GPS)]]** — precision guidance, navigation, timing
2. **[[SATCOM Architecture|SATCOM]]** — beyond-line-of-sight (BLOS) communications
3. **[[Missile Warning and Tracking]]** — infrared launch detection and midcourse tracking
4. **Space-Based [[SIGINT and ELINT Overview|ISR]]** — imagery, SIGINT, ELINT from orbit
5. **[[Space Domain Awareness]]** — track objects, detect threats
6. **Environmental Monitoring** — weather, space weather

> These six are **interdependent**. Disrupting any one degrades the others.

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> These six areas map directly to joint force functions — when an adversary targets a satellite, think about which joint function they're trying to degrade, not just which satellite they're targeting. A strike on a SATCOM satellite is an attack on C2; a strike on GPS is an attack on precision fires and manoeuvre.

## Space-Based ISR

- Collection types: EO (visible/IR imagery), SAR (all-weather), SIGINT (comms intercept), ELINT (radar characterisation)
- NRO operates classified national constellation
- Commercial providers: Planet (daily global), Maxar (high-res), Capella (SAR)
- China assessed to have achieved global ISR constellation coverage (1,060+ satellites by mid-2025)
- ISR orbits: [[Orbital Mechanics|LEO]] polar / sun-synchronous (~97°) for consistent lighting
- Adversaries time camouflage and deception to satellite overpass windows
