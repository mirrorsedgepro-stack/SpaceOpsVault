# ADF SATCOM Systems

> [!abstract] Quick Summary
> Covers Australia's own SATCOM assets — Optus C1, IS-22, and the SPA 9102 terminal — and how they fit into the broader joint SATCOM architecture. Critical reading for understanding Australia's sovereign SATCOM position and the capability gaps approaching in the late 2020s.

## Optus C1 — Australia's Sovereign Satellite

- Australia's **only sovereign** (Australian-owned) satellite
- [[Orbital Mechanics|GEO]] at 156°E
- Defence payload: X/Ka-band (wideband) + UHF (narrowband)
- Design life expired ~2018; currently in **inclined orbit** (~2027 end of life)
- Strategically significant as the only capacity Australia owns **independently of allied decisions**

> [!tip] Hot Tip
> Optus C1 and IS-22 are commercial satellites with no inherent military protection — they are the most jamming-vulnerable elements of ADF SATCOM. Plan for their degradation in any high-end scenario and ensure alternate SATCOM pathways (WGS, MUOS) are coordinated in advance.

## Intelsat IS-22

- ADF-owned UHF payload on a commercial satellite (launched 2012)
- 20 UHF channels; Indian Ocean region
- Contract through approximately 2027–2030
- Surplus channels shared with US (Indian Ocean); ADF receives US UHF capacity over Pacific

## SPA 9102 (Sovereign Persistent Access)

- Original **JP 9102** (dedicated GEO satellites, ~$7–12B AUD) **cancelled November 2023**
  - Single-orbit GEO assessed too vulnerable
  - AUKUS/DSR budget pressure also a factor
- Rescoped as **SPA 9102** focusing on narrowband SATCOM
- RFI issued mid-2025

### Capability Gap Risk
- Must replace **Optus C1 UHF** (~2027) and **IS-22** (~late 2020s)
- Capability gap risk if not through Second Pass in time

### Four ADF SATCOM Focus Areas
1. **Resilience** — survivability in contested environment
2. **Sovereignty** — Australian-controlled capacity
3. **Interoperability** — Five Eyes compatible
4. **Commercial leverage** — exploit commercial SATCOM market

---

> [!warning]- Constraints, Limitations and Assumptions
> **Constraints:** Optus C1 is a commercial asset operated under a government agreement — its prioritisation relative to commercial traffic in a crisis is untested and not contractually guaranteed.
>
> **Limitations:** Commercial satellites have no anti-jam capability, no COMSEC at the space layer, and limited manoeuvre capability. Both Optus C1 and IS-22 are approaching end of life with no confirmed replacement on current timelines.
>
> **Assumptions:** Assumes commercial satellite operators will prioritise ADF traffic in a conflict — this is not guaranteed. Assumes SPA 9102 achieves Second Pass approval in time to bridge the post-2027 capability gap.
